Movement Timeline

2019

  • 1.03 Million Anti Extradition Bill Protest

    This day is the start of the movement. 1.3 million Hongkongers gathered outside the Central Government Offices to express their objection to the Extradition Bill, which would allow the transfer of fugitives to mainland China. This bill is active to everyone that no matter which country citizens they are.

    2019/06/09

  • Police shot tear gases into CITIC tower, due to a big strike that day

    40,000 protesters gathered outside the Government Headquarters attempted and successfully stalled the second reading of the bill, though the Police deployed numerous canisters of tear gas, rubber bullets and bean bag rounds to disperse the protesters.

    (Sources from wiki)

    2019/06/12

  • Government announced suspension to the Extradition Bill

    At a press conference, the Hong Kong Government has announced the suspension of the Extradition Bill. But on a legal level, the word "suspension" does not have any legal binding power which causing Hongkongers still depressed about this announcement. On the same day, a teenager committed suicide. He was holding up a sign about the Anti Extradition Bill before falling off a building.

    2019/06/15

  • Movement escalating, the occupation of the legislative council.

    The first of July is the day of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region Establishment Day. Most of the Hongkongers decided to march from Central to the Legislative council to protest for the Extradition Bill. When the night has come, a group of protesters decided to occupy the Legislative building. While the occupation, they flew a blackened Bauhinia Flag (Hong Kong Flag) to express the legislative council's undemocratic nature.

    2019/07/01

  • The Government claimed the bill is "dead"

    Carrie Lam, who is currently the Chief Executive of Hong Kong, has claimed that the Extradition Bill is "dead". Although she has announced it officially through a press conference, most Hongkongers are still angry and keep protesting for undemocratic Hong Kong.

    2019/07/09

  • Hong Kong Police has forcefully charged into a shopping mall and dispersed protesters

    A lot of protesters marched at a district called Sha Tin. In the evening, the police have chosen to flank the protesters from three sides which the protesters have to flee into a shopping mall to avoid getting arrested. Unusually, riot police charged into the mall and trapped passersby in it with starting to use pepper spray.

    2019/07/14

  • Sixth march of the Extradition Bill

    The march on 21st July was the sixth biggest march after the Extradition Bill has announced. In the evening, riot police armed escalated using tons of pepper spray, tear gas, and rubber bullets to disperse protesters.


    Yuen Long Triads Attack

    Simultaneously, some of the protesters have chosen to take the MTR train to go home. They would never think of experiencing an attack organized by triads in Yuen Long Train Station. Nonetheless, many people inside the train are just normal passenger but still being involved in this attack. The police have arrived at the scene 39 minutes after the triad attack. Most of the Hongkongers determined that they were cohort with the triads because there is a police station near the Yuen Long Train Station which does not need more than 20 minutes even if they walk.

    2019/07/21

  • First "Fly to Freedom" rally held in the Hong Kong International Airport

    Some protesters have organized an event called "Fly to Freedom" on some online platforms to gather Hong Kong civilians to take a peaceful rally inside the airport.

    2019/07/26

  • Rally continues in different districts, police fired tear gases in residential areas

    There are different rallies held in different districts and areas which some of the residents surrounded the police station also. In the late evening, most of the residents are still protesting which the police selected to fired tear gases over 60 times inside the residential areas. Cases of children inhaling tear gas were reported.

    2019/08/04

  • City-wide strikes organized in every district in Hong Kong

    Many adults decided to take a strike with supporting the whole movement and teenage protester in this day. Most of them have taken rallies in every district in Hong Kong.

    2019/08/05

  • Police fired a bean bag round at a teenage first aider's head

    Unrest continues this day. A teenage girl First-aider got hit on the head by a bean bag round by the riot police. Her protective goggles have been destroyed, and she suffered severe facial trauma with her right eye and maxilla bone. It caused permanent vision loss to her right eye.

    2019/08/11

  • Over 1.7 million people participated in a mass demonstration under heavy rain

    1.7 million citizens and protesters gathered in Victoria Park for a "flow-water" rally while the police issued a Letter of Objection to the march. This was the seventh big march since the announcement of the Extradition Bill.

    2019/08/18

  • City-wide human chain

    Hong Kong protesters organized a human chain in Hong Kong, with over 135000 people joining hands across districts to express their will to freedom.

    2019/08/23

  • Hong Kong police dressed as protesters, shooting their back with pepper bullets

    Many police posed as protesters dressed in dark clothing, gas masks, and holding pepper bullet guns to shoot at the protesters to arrest them.


    Riot police breached into the Prince Edward Train Station and attack civilians

    Inside the Prince Edward Train Station, some protesters argued with a few middle-aged men. After the quarrel, a group of Special Tactical Squad riot police breached into the MTR train cabin and started to assault every single person using batons and pepper spray. They did not arrest any people but only beaten the passengers.

    2019/08/31

  • Schools day started, joint-school strikes by all Hong Kong Universities and Hundreds of Secondary School student

    Although the school day has started at the start of September, the Anti-Extradition Bill movement remains intense which most Hong Kong students have chosen to take a strike on the whole September.

    2019/09

  • International Global Anti-Totalitarianism March

    929 Global Anti-Totalitarianism March was a global march in many regions, including the United Kingdom, United States of America, Australia, Taiwan etc. This is the day when the Hong Kong movement has gone international.


    Indonesian Journalist injured by Hong Kong Police

    During the 929 Global Anti-Totalitarianism March in Hong Kong, an Indonesian Journalist who was working for the Indonesian media "SUARA" was shot in the right eye by rubber bullet. She has been sent to the hospital immediately, but her right eye was confirmed as permanently blinded.

    2019/09/29

  • Teenage protester nearly got killed on Chinese National Day

    Hong Kong citizens took rallies and marches in different districts on the Chinese National Day with the slogan "No National Day, Only National Shame". A teenage protester nearly got killed by the Hong Kong Police after he got shot in the chest by a live round bullet. At the evening, riot police kept using tear gases to disperse street protesters.

    2019/10/01

  • Siege of the Chinese University of Hong Kong

    The riot police and tactical units confront students at one of the higher education institutes - the Chinese University of Hong Kong, by firing massive tear gases and bean bag rounds. The confrontation continues even after nightfall. The whole protest was ended peacefully which the police did not breach into the University.

    2019/11/12

  • Siege of the Polytechnic University

    Unlike the Siege of the Chinese University of Hong Kong, after two days of confrontation between the police and the protesters inside the Polytechnic University, the police tried to breach into the university's facilities and block all the way out. Therefore, the trapped protesters had to find some special ways to get out to avoid being arrested. There were 1377 people got arrested in this siege.

    2019/11/16

  • Statistic from 9th June 2019 - 11th December 2020

    The total number of arrested: 10,171.
    The total number of being charged: 2389.

    (Source from HK01)
